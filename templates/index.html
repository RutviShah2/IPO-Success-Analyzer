What is an IPO?

An Initial Public Offering (IPO) is when a private company offers its shares to the public for the first time on a stock exchange. It allows the company to raise capital from public investors while giving ordinary people the opportunity to participate in a company's growth story.

🏦

Issue Size

Total money a company plans to raise from the IPO in crores (₹ Cr).

💰

Offer Price

The price at which shares are offered to investors before listing on exchange.

📈

Subscription

How many times the IPO was subscribed — higher means more investor demand.

🏛️

QIB

Qualified Institutional Buyers — banks, mutual funds, insurance companies.

💼

HNI

High Net-worth Individuals — wealthy investors applying for large lots.

👥

RII

Retail Individual Investors — general public applying for small lots (up to ₹2 lakh).

Why IPO Prediction Matters

Every year, hundreds of IPOs are listed on Indian exchanges. Not all deliver gains on listing day. Predicting IPO success helps investors make smarter decisions:

  • 🎯 Retail investors make informed decisions before applying to avoid losses
  • 📉 Identify IPOs with high risk of listing below offer price before committing capital
  • 🤖 Use data-driven insights based on subscription patterns instead of speculation
  • 📊 Understand institutional confidence through QIB, HNI, and RII subscription analysis
  • 🔮 Leverage historical patterns using machine learning to predict future performance

Key Factors Affecting IPO Success

🏛️

QIB Subscription

High institutional interest (10×+) is the strongest positive signal. Institutions do deep due diligence before investing.

📊

Total Subscription

Overall demand vs. supply. An IPO subscribed 30× or more typically lists with a listing gain.

💹

Sector Performance

IPOs in high-growth sectors like Technology, Pharma, and Finance tend to perform better.

💰

Offer Price Band

Lower-priced IPOs attract more retail participation. Very high prices can reduce demand.

📏

Issue Size

Smaller issues tend to list at higher premiums due to limited supply vs. high demand.

📅

Market Conditions

Bull markets see more successful IPOs. Macro conditions, interest rates, and sentiment matter.
